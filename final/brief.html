Final Project Design Brief

For my final project, I will be implementing my Every Picture project with better quality of images with more interactions.

Project Goals

My goal for this project is to bring more attention to the collage of film images I made and present individual images with a description in a user friendly way. One thing that wasn’t accomplished in my Every Picture project was showcasing a high resolution images, especially for the collage image. In order to fix this, I plan to scan my image again to a .iff file format with 300 dpi whereas my first one was scanned as a jpg file and place it with a larger width and height because there’s a big difference when the image is small and big.

Design Strategies

want to create a different, better layout of the overall design so that the users can feel more connected and understand my film journey. I will be showing the collage image in a bigger dimension and each image would slide in from the side when it is clicked. I want to add some animation so that the transition can be smooth and interactive. I will be keeping the font that I used earlier. Since I am going to add some short descriptions for each image, I will be also focusing on typography hierarchy.

Interaction Strategies

The main interaction this project has is clicking each sections of thumbnails. In order to encourage users to interact with the project, I want to improve on how to make those sections stand out so that the users know they can click them and see what happens next. As I stated earlier, I am also planning to add some animation to make the visual more interesting and cohesive.

Experience Goals

I hope to show my passion in film photography to the users who visit my website. I hope they can find the charms in film photos. By clicking each thumbnail and observing full images, I want to show my viewpoint/perspective and what I see. Not all pictures have a special story, but I hope they get to me better while having fun exploring my project.
